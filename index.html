01 / About

A physicist's curiosity

I'm Hafsa Kamal, a Pakistani physicist who has just completed a Master's in Nanotechnology at the University of Twente, with a research focus on scanning probe microscopy and the design of micro/nano devices.

My research lives at the intersection of fundamental physics and applied engineering. I've designed minimally invasive cortisol biosensors, optimized mechanical ring resonators in Clewin, studied twisted bilayer graphene, and most recently completed a Master's thesis on the electronic and structural properties of bismuth on silicon carbide using STM.

I'm always curious to learn new things — moving between 2D materials, MEMS, and now high-voltage characterization has shown me how much physics rewards a generalist's eye. I'm now seeking a PhD position where I can commit deeply to a research question and build a thesis worthy of the time it deserves.

02 / Research

Selected research

01
M.Sc. Thesis · 2024 — 2026

Electronic & Structural Properties of Bi on SiC

Master's thesis investigating ultra-thin bismuth films on silicon carbide using Scanning Tunneling Microscopy. The work probes how dimensional confinement reshapes electronic states — the STM image to the right reveals the characteristic anisotropic chain structure of Bi on a stepped SiC surface, laying groundwork for spintronics and quantum-material platforms.

Team Project · 2025

μDr. Sensor — Cortisol Biosensor

Designed a minimally invasive micro-device for measuring cortisol concentration in animals to monitor stress levels reliably. Led design of mechanical housing and integration of sensing elements; the prototype was 3D-printed and validated in the lab.

CAD & Mechanical · 2025

Sensor Housing — Mechanical Design

3D mechanical design of the μDr. Sensor housing: cavities, electrode pads, and a release-etched structure for minimally invasive deployment. Iterated on geometry to balance manufacturability with sensing performance.

Mechanical Resonators · 2025

Mechanical Ring Resonator

Designed and optimized a mechanical ring resonator in Clewin, alongside the full mask layout for fabrication. The chip targets resonant micro-mechanical sensing — exploring quality factor, mode shapes, and damping trade-offs at the micro-scale.

Layout Engineering · 2025

Clewin Mask Design

Authored multi-layer photolithography mask designs in Clewin for sensor and resonator devices. Defined layer-stack ordering, alignment marks, and exposure-region geometries — translating physics intent into fab-ready GDS layouts.

Lab-on-a-Chip for Fetal Growth Restriction

Collaborated on a microfluidic lab-on-a-chip device for early monitoring of Fetal Growth Restriction. Contributed to system architecture and biosensing strategy — translating clinical need into a device-level specification.
